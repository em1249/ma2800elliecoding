Statue of Liberty

The Statue of Liberty, designed by Frédéric Auguste Bartholdi and engineered by Gustave Eiffel, was a gift from France to the U.S. proposed in 1865 by Édouard de Laboulaye to honor America's independence and friendship. Dedicated on October 28, 1886, on Bedloe's Island, the 151-foot copper statue, representing liberty and democracy, was shipped in 350 pieces from France.
